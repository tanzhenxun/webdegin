Vesak Day

Vesak is a holiday traditionally observed by Buddhists in South Asia and Southeast Asia as well as Tibet and Mongolia. The festival commemorates the birth, enlightenment (Nibbāna), and death (Parinirvāna) of Gautama Buddha in Theravada, Tibetan Buddhism and Navayana.

Vesak Day actually commemorates the birth, enlightenment and nirvana of Sakyamuni Buddha (Buddha) on the same day. According to records, the Buddha was born on the full moon day in May; 35 years later, on a full moon day in May, he attained enlightenment under the Bodhi tree; later, the day of his Nirvana coincided with the full moon day in May. Although the three are not the same day, they are all in May, and have the biggest common feature of a full moon. Therefore, later generations of Buddhists set the first full moon day in May every year to celebrate Vesak Day.

In the East Asian tradition, a celebration of Buddha's Birthday typically occurs around the traditional timing of Vesak, while the Buddha's awakening and passing away are celebrated as separate holidays that occur at other times in the calendar as Bodhi Day and Nibbāna Day.

The month of May usually has one full moon, but as there are 29.5 days between full moons, occasionally there are two.

Bath to the infant Buddha

Since ancient times, Buddhists all over the world celebrate Buddha’s birthday by using fragrant water to bathe the image of the infant Buddha. There is great significance in the act as the fragrant water is poured over the statue of the infant Buddha three times. parade.

Procession

Procession

Buddhists in Malaysia will hold a parade of floats on the streets of Kuala Lumpur on Vesak Day. This activity usually lasts for several hours. One of the floats will have a huge Buddha statue, and believers will accompany the floats with flowers and candles.

Hari Raya Aidilfitri

Hari Raya Aidilfitri is a festival celebrated by Muslims all over the world regardless of whether they are Arabs, English, Malays, Indian-Muslims, Chinese-Muslims and other nations around the world who are Muslim to mark the end of the month of Ramadan which Muslims Islam has diligently practiced fasting.

The origin of this holiday can be traced all the way back to the Islamic prophet Muhammad. He began this festival after he migrated to Mecca. In the region, the Muslim religion was firmly established by the 15th century. Hari Raya Aidilfitri has been celebrated since that time.

Activities

Puasa (Ramadan)

Puasa (Ramadan)

Ramadan is the name of the one month fasting period when Muslims fast daily for a period of almost 12 hours from dawn till sunset.

Dress up New Cloth

Dress up New Cloth

Muslims also tend to dress up for the occasion. Women wear the baju kurung (traditional Malay dress), baju kebaya (traditional blouse-dress), or a jubah (long one-piece dress), while men typically don a songkok (traditional hat) and the baju Melayu (traditional men’s wear).

Open House

Open House

On this special occasion, you may wish to attend state-organized open houses. Depending on the state, this may be hosted at the Sultan’s palace, a state-owned building, or even an open public land.

Give Green Envelope

Give Green Envelope

It is a Malay adaptation of the Chinese red envelope custom. During the festival of Eid ul-Fitr, Muslims in Malaysia, Brunei, Singapore, and Indonesia hand out money in green envelopes to guests who visit their homes.
